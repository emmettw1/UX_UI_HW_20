Promising Pages

About

Promising Pages is a non-profit located in Charlotte, NC. Their mission is to provide books to the estimated 60,000 kids in the Charlotte area who live in a book desert, meaning they have access to few, if any, books at home. In partnership with community organizations, they have shared over one and a half million books.

Promising Pages hopes to increase child literacy in the Charlotte, NC area. By comparing literacy data from before and after the pandemic, we have observed that child literacy rates in the Charlotte area have dropped by over 20%. Promising Pages’ goal to address this problem is to provide books to kids to help boost their interest in reading, improve their vocabulary, and self esteem.

How can we improve the website to encourage people to donate books, their time, as well as monetary resources in order to increase kids’ access and interest in books?

Future Prospects

After further user testing, we received valuable feedback such as modifying the transitions for a smoother user experience, adding a working menu, and modifying the language for greater clarity. We also would like to conduct A/B testing to examine color choices, expand the ability to view book wishlists, redesign with greater accessibility, and fully implement material design.

Final Thoughts

This project was eye-opening work regarding the problem of children's access to books. Through reviewing Promising Pages' website and interacting with potential users, we learned that both ourselves and others underestimated the problem. In future iterations, we would focus on adding and expanding features that increate interactivity and involvement from the donors and children impacted. Through greater participation on all ends, Promising Pages can continue to acheive its goal of remediating the Charlotte, NC-area book desert.
